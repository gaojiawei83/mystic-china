Feng Shui

Feng Shui Basics: 10 Simple Tips to Harmonize Your Home

📅 February 1, 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read
Advertisement

Feng Shui (风水), which literally translates to "Wind and Water," is an ancient Chinese practice that has been shaping homes, cities, and lives for over 3,000 years. At its core, Feng Shui is the art of arranging your environment to maximize the flow of positive energy (Qi) and create harmony between you and your surroundings.

You don't need to be an expert or spend a fortune to benefit from Feng Shui. In this guide, we'll cover the fundamental principles and share 10 simple, actionable tips you can implement in your home today.

What Is Feng Shui?

Feng Shui is based on the observation that our environment profoundly affects our well-being, relationships, and success. Just as a plant thrives in the right conditions — proper sunlight, good soil, clean water — humans also flourish when their environment supports them energetically.

The practice is built on several key principles:

  • Qi (气): The vital life force energy that flows through everything. In your home, Qi should flow gently and smoothly — not too fast (creating chaos) and not too slow (creating stagnation).
  • Yin and Yang: The balance of active and passive energies. A home needs both — bright, active spaces (Yang) and quiet, restful spaces (Yin).
  • The Five Elements: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water. Each brings different qualities to a space, and they should be balanced.
  • The Bagua Map: An energy map that divides a space into nine areas, each corresponding to a different aspect of life — wealth, fame, relationships, family, health, creativity, knowledge, career, and helpful people.
💡 Key Insight: Feng Shui is not about superstition. It's about creating environments that feel good, function well, and support your goals — principles that align with modern psychology and design thinking.

10 Simple Feng Shui Tips for Your Home

1. Clear the Clutter

This is the most important Feng Shui tip of all. Clutter blocks the flow of Qi and keeps you stuck in the past. Go through each room and remove anything that you don't use, don't love, or that carries negative associations. Pay special attention to:

  • Items stuffed under the bed
  • Overflowing closets
  • Broken objects you've been meaning to fix
  • Papers and mail piled up on surfaces

2. Fix What's Broken

In Feng Shui, broken things represent broken energy. A leaking faucet symbolizes money flowing away. A burnt-out light bulb represents a dimming of your clarity or vitality. A squeaky door creates irritation every time it's used. Fix these small things — the energetic payoff is significant.

3. Position Your Bed Correctly (The Command Position)

Your bed is the most important piece of furniture in Feng Shui because you spend roughly one-third of your life in it. Place your bed in the command position:

  • You can see the door from your bed (but you are not directly in line with it)
  • Your bed has a solid headboard against a wall (support and stability)
  • There is space on both sides of the bed (balance in relationships)
  • Avoid placing your bed under a window or exposed beams

4. Create a Welcoming Entrance

Your front door is called the "Mouth of Qi" — it's where energy enters your home. Make it inviting:

  • Keep the entrance clean and well-lit
  • Make sure the door opens fully (nothing stored behind it)
  • Add a welcome mat and some greenery
  • Fix any squeaky hinges or sticking locks immediately

5. Bring in Living Plants

Plants represent the Wood element and bring vibrant, growing energy into your home. They purify the air and lift the energy of any space. Best Feng Shui plants include:

  • Money Plant (Pachira): Associated with wealth and prosperity
  • Peace Lily: Purifies air and brings calming energy
  • Lucky Bamboo: Symbolizes flexibility and resilience
  • Snake Plant: Protective energy, great for bedrooms

Avoid dead or dying plants — they drag the energy down. And avoid sharp, spiky plants in main living areas.

💡 Key Insight: If you can't keep real plants alive, high-quality artificial plants are acceptable. The point is to have the visual symbol of vibrant life and growth in your space.

6. Balance the Five Elements

Each room should have a balance of the Five Elements. Here's how to introduce each one:

  • Wood: Plants, green colors, rectangular shapes, floral patterns
  • Fire: Candles, red/orange colors, triangular shapes, lighting, animals
  • Earth: Crystals, pottery, yellow/brown colors, square shapes, ceramic items
  • Metal: Metal objects, white/gray colors, round shapes, rocks and stones
  • Water: Fountains, mirrors, black/blue colors, wavy shapes, glass items

7. Use Mirrors Wisely

Mirrors are powerful Feng Shui tools — they reflect and expand energy. Use them thoughtfully:

  • DO place a mirror to reflect a beautiful view or natural light into a room
  • DO use mirrors to make small spaces feel larger
  • DON'T place a mirror directly facing your bed (it can disturb sleep)
  • DON'T place a mirror directly facing the front door (it pushes energy out)
  • DON'T use broken, chipped, or distorted mirrors

8. Keep the Bathroom Door Closed

In Feng Shui, bathrooms represent draining energy — water literally flows out of the home through drains. To prevent wealth and health energy from "draining away":

  • Always keep the bathroom door closed
  • Keep the toilet lid down when not in use
  • Fix any drips or leaks immediately
  • Keep the bathroom clean and well-maintained

9. Create a Wealth Corner

Using the Bagua map, the wealth area of your home (or any room) is the far-left corner from the entrance. Enhance this area with:

  • Purple, gold, or red accents
  • A healthy plant (especially a money plant)
  • Something that represents abundance to you (a beautiful bowl, a piece of art, a vision board)
  • Good lighting — wealth energy thrives in bright spaces

10. Let in Natural Light and Fresh Air

Light and air are the most fundamental carriers of Qi. A dark, stuffy room has stagnant energy. To improve this:

  • Open windows daily, even for just 10 minutes
  • Use full-spectrum light bulbs where natural light is limited
  • Clean your windows regularly (dirty windows block clarity)
  • Use air-purifying plants
  • Consider an essential oil diffuser with uplifting scents like citrus or peppermint

Common Feng Shui Mistakes to Avoid

  • Clutter under the bed: This disturbs your sleep and blocks energy flow around your most restorative space.
  • Desk facing a wall: This represents hitting obstacles in your career. Position your desk so you can see the door.
  • Sharp corners pointing at you: This creates "poison arrows" (Sha Qi) — aggressive energy. Soften corners with plants or fabric.
  • Too much red in the bedroom: Red is a very active, fiery color. In the bedroom, it can disturb rest and create relationship tension. Use it sparingly.
  • TV or exercise equipment in the bedroom: The bedroom should be for rest and intimacy. Electronics and workout gear bring active Yang energy that disrupts sleep.
"Feng Shui is not just about moving furniture. It's about moving energy — and when you move energy, you move your life." — Chinese Proverb

Start Small, Feel the Difference

Feng Shui doesn't require a complete home renovation. Start with one tip — perhaps clearing clutter or repositioning your bed — and notice how it feels. As you gradually implement more changes, you'll likely find that your home not only looks better but feels dramatically different.

The ultimate goal of Feng Shui is not a picture-perfect home. It's a home that supports you — your health, your relationships, your work, and your dreams. When your environment is aligned, life flows more smoothly. That is the true gift of this ancient Chinese wisdom.
