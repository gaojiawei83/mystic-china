Here are 10 of its most powerful quotes, each with the original Chinese text and a detailed explanation.

1. еӨ©иЎҢеҒҘпјҢеҗӣеӯҗд»ҘиҮӘејәдёҚжҒ?/h2>

"Heaven moves with strength. The superior person ceaselessly strengthens themselves."

вҖ?Xiang Zhuan, Hexagram 1 (Qian / Heaven)

The single most quoted line in the I Ching. It teaches that self-improvement is not a destination but a lifelong practice вҖ?like the sun, you rise every day and do the work.

еҮәиҮӘгҖҠиұЎдј В·д№ҫеҚҰгҖӢгҖӮиў«еј•з”ЁжңҖеӨҡзҡ„жҳ“з»ҸеҗҚеҸҘгҖӮиҮӘжҲ‘жҸҗеҚҮдёҚжҳҜз»ҲзӮ№пјҢиҖҢжҳҜз»Ҳиә«дҝ®иЎҢвҖ”вҖ”еҰӮеӨӘйҳіпјҢжҜҸеӨ©еҚҮиө·пјҢжҜҸеӨ©дҝ®иЎҢгҖ?/div>

2. ең°еҠҝеқӨпјҢеҗӣеӯҗд»ҘеҺҡеҫ·иҪҪзү?/h2>

"The Earth's condition is receptive devotion. The superior person carries all things with generous virtue."

вҖ?Xiang Zhuan, Hexagram 2 (Kun / Earth)

The companion to quote #1. If Heaven teaches relentless effort, Earth teaches boundless generosity. True strength is not just about pushing forward вҖ?it's about holding space for others.

еҮәиҮӘгҖҠиұЎдј В·еқӨеҚҰгҖӢгҖӮеҰӮдёҠеҸҘзҡ„дјҙеҸҘгҖӮеӨ©ж•ҷдәәеҘӢиҝӣпјҢең°ж•ҷдәәеҢ…е®№гҖӮзңҹжӯЈзҡ„еҠӣйҮҸдёҚд»…жҳҜжҺЁиҝӣвҖ”вҖ”жӣҙжҳҜдёәд»–дәәз•ҷеҮәз©әй—ҙгҖ?/div>
еҺҡеҫ·иҪҪзү© (Hou De Zai Wu) вҖ?"Carry all things with generous virtue." This idiom is used in modern Chinese to describe a person of great moral character who can bear heavy responsibilities with grace and compassion, just as the Earth supports all living beings without complaint.

3. дёҖйҳҙдёҖйҳід№Ӣи°“йҒ“

"One Yin, one Yang вҖ?this is called the Tao."

вҖ?Xi Ci Zhuan, Part 1, Chapter 5

The philosophical heart of the entire I Ching. The Tao is not a static thing вҖ?it is the rhythm of alternation. Inhale, exhale. Day, night. Activity, rest. This pulse is the very nature of reality.

4. жҳ“жңүеӨӘжһҒпјҢжҳҜз”ҹдёӨд»?/h2>

"In the Changes there is the Supreme Ultimate, which gives rise to the Two Forms."

вҖ?Xi Ci Zhuan, Part 1, Chapter 11

The I Ching's creation theory. From the limitless void (Wuji) arises the first distinction (Taiji), which unfolds into Yin and Yang вҖ?and from there, the entire universe. A binary cosmology written 2,500 years before computers.

еҮәиҮӘгҖҠзі»иҫһдј дёҠВ·з¬¬еҚҒдёҖз« гҖӢгҖӮжҳ“з»Ҹзҡ„еҲӣдё–и®әгҖӮд»Һж— жһҒеҲ°еӨӘжһҒпјҢеӨӘжһҒз”ҹдёӨд»ӘпјҢдёӨд»ӘеҢ–дёҮзү©гҖӮеңЁи®Ўз®—жңәеҮәзҺ°еүҚ2500е№ҙеҶҷдёӢзҡ„дәҢе…ғе®Үе®ҷи®әгҖ?/div>

5. з©·еҲҷеҸҳпјҢеҸҳеҲҷйҖҡпјҢйҖҡеҲҷд№?/h2>

"When pushed to the limit, one changes. Through change, one finds passage. Through passage, one endures."

вҖ?Xi Ci Zhuan, Part 2, Chapter 2

A complete philosophy of change in 8 Chinese characters. When you hit a wall, don't keep pushing вҖ?change. Adaptation is not weakness; it's the secret of survival. Everything that endures does so through constant transformation.

еҮәиҮӘгҖҠзі»иҫһдј дёӢВ·з¬¬дәҢз« гҖӢгҖӮе…«дёӘеӯ—зҡ„е®Ңж•ҙеҸҳйқ©е“ІеӯҰгҖӮзў°еЈҒж—¶пјҢдёҚиҰҒз»§з»ӯзЎ¬ж’һвҖ”вҖ”иҰҒеҸҳгҖӮйҖӮеә”дёҚжҳҜиҪҜејұпјҢиҖҢжҳҜз”ҹеӯҳзҡ„з§ҳиҜҖгҖӮеҮЎиғҪй•ҝд№…зҡ„пјҢеҝ…йҖҡиҝҮжҢҒз»ӯзҡ„иҪ¬еҢ–гҖ?/div>
з©·еҲҷеҸҳпјҢеҸҳеҲҷйҖҡпјҢйҖҡеҲҷд№?(Qiong Ze Bian, Bian Ze Tong, Tong Ze Jiu) вҖ?A complete philosophy of adaptation in three stages: (1) When exhausted, change вҖ?don't persist in a failing strategy. (2) When you change, you find a way through the obstacle. (3) When you find passage, you achieve lasting endurance. This applies to businesses, relationships, health, and personal growth.

вҖ?Xiang Zhuan, Hexagram 63 (Ji Ji / After Completion)

The I Ching's wisdom on prevention. Most people wait for disaster to strike before they respond. The wise person anticipates problems and prevents them before they arise. An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure вҖ?written in ancient China.

вҖ?Xi Ci Zhuan, Part 1, Chapter 8
